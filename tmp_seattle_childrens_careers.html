Living in Seattle

Seattle Children’s is proud to call the Pacific Northwest home. Many dream of having the opportunity to live and work here. Our employees live that dream.

We invite you to learn more about the award-winning schools, diverse neighborhoods, extensive public transportation, arts communities and outdoor recreation that make Seattle a fantastic place to live and work.

We look forward to seeing you in the Pacific Northwest!

Seattle skyline

Located on the Puget Sound, Seattle is a dynamic urban area surrounded by natural beauty

Seattle waterfront

The Seattle waterfront has many attractions including an aquarium, Ferris wheel, and plenty of shopping and restaurants

Schools and universites

Seattle Children’s partners with the University of Washington and is close to a number of world-class universities.

Shopping

Seattle has a thriving shopping scene, including Pike Place Market, a year-round farmers market in the heart of downtown

Ferry system

Ferries departing from the Seattle area give access to the islands of Puget Sound and the Olympic Peninsula

Family-friendly

Arts and music

Ranked Among the Best Children’s Hospitals in the Nation

Because of our people, Seattle Children’s is recognized as a leading teaching, research and specialty center at the forefront of pediatric care.

Our goal is to be the country’s #1 children’s hospital. We seek quality people with the right skills. We look for team players who want to work at the top of their skill level and continually improve the care we provide.

U.S. News & World Report has recognized Seattle Children’s as one of the top 10 best children’s hospitals in the U.S. and the best in the Pacific Northwest. We have been named as a top children’s hospital every year since they began ranking medical facilities more than 30 years ago.

Seattle Children’s is the only children’s hospital in the Northwest to be designated as a Magnet organization. We received this recognition from the American Nurses Credentialing Center in 2008, and again in 2013, 2018 and 2023. Click to learn more (nursingworld.org).

Forbes named Seattle Children’s to the 2026 America’s Best Large Employers list. This recognition is based on employee feedback from independent surveys.

Seattle Children’s is verified as a Level I Children’s Surgery Center by the American College of Surgeons (ACS), and the first children’s hospital in Washington to earn this status. Click to learn more (facs.org).

Seattle Children’s received a three-year recertification as an ISO 9001 organization. Provided by the International Organization for Standardization, this certification provides validation of clinical quality standards. Children’s is one of the few children’s hospitals in the world to earn this certification.

The Human Rights Campaign Foundation has designated Seattle Children's as an LGBTQ+ Healthcare Equality Leader. The Healthcare Equality Index (HEI) is the national LGBTQ+ benchmarking tool that evaluates healthcare facilities’ policies and practices. Click to learn more (hrc.org).

For 2022, Seattle Children's has received the Gold ELSO Award for Excellence in Life Support. The ELSO Excellence in Life Support Award recognizes ECLS programs worldwide that distinguish themselves by having processes, procedures and systems in place that promote exceptional care. Click to learn more (elso.org).

Seattle Children’s has been recognized as a Center of Excellence in Pediatric Sedation by the Society for Pediatric Sedation. This national recognition reflects our commitment to safe, high-quality sedation care that supports the best possible experience for your child.
